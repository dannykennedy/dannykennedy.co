Hello everyone,

What a year! Sorry that our newsletter has been a little quiet recently while we’ve been busy on projects. I don’t know about you, but we’re happy to close the books on 2018-19 and get down to some fresh and exciting work.

We feel that going into 2019-20, the role of data vis designers and developers is only becoming more important. People are wary about spin, deepfakes and the reliability of online news, and it’s up to us not just to show facts and figures, but to give the context behind them. Andrea was part of a panel discussion at Gallery 4A recently that discussed a lot of these issues. In many ways data visualisation itself is becoming more common, and our role is expanding - it’s not purely about making graphics, but about applying design thinking to data problems.

One thing we’re looking forward to this year is the chance to work at the intersection between design and science. We’ve been working with biotech not-for-profit Iggy Get Out to visualise multi-omics data, and we’ve been designing a staff scheduling chatbot powered by IBM’s Watson AI. We need to keep up with the changing landscape of data engineering, but at the same time it’s more important than ever to find the right balance between human and machine, both in terms of ethics and design.

Have a look at some of our latest projects below, and we look forward to catching up in 2019-20.

Andrea and Jack, Small Multiples Directors

MULTI-OMICS VISUALISER
Iggy Get Out

Small Multiples partnered with the pioneering research team at Iggy Get Out, which aims to study neurodegenerative diseases using a whole body network approach, combining and visualising multiple omics data sets.

MAPPING EVERY VOTE
The Guardian

The federal election set the tone for a lot of this year, surprised a lot of people and raised a lot of questions. We tried to answer some of them by visualising the results with one dot for every vote, a visualisation which was featured in the Guardian.
